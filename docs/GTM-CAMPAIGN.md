# GTM Campaign: the Piyush machine, localized for every market

**Written:** 6 July 2026. Fourth companion to `GTM-PLAYBOOK.md`,
`GTM-AUDIENCE-RESEARCH.md`, `GTM-STORY-BANK.md`.

**The idea: one machine, many dialects.** A single campaign architecture, a hero
commercial feeding a daily three-pillar content engine, localized into each
market's emotional grammar. Same human truth, spoken the way that culture actually
feels it. That is Piyush Pandey's real lesson: not a style, cultural fluency.

**The production line (one person can run it):** BD talent performs the real
emotion in the target market's *register* and mimics the mouth shapes → AI
face-swap makes them read as that nationality → AI extends the world / set →
ElevenLabs supplies the native voice and dialect (the actor only mouths it). One
afternoon, one grey wall, one SLR, seven-plus markets.

---

## The one truth (the spine under everything)

> The exam tests whether you'd make the right call, not what you memorised. Dumps
> train recall; we train judgment. Know you're ready before you pay to find out.

**Every asset, in every market, ends on one CTA: the free exam simulation.** The
story creates the fear; the readiness score is the salesman.

## The machine: one hero + three daily pillars

- **The Commercial (the anthem).** A 60-90s hero film per market, the emotional
  peak. Pinned/launch piece and the paid-boost asset. Built from the market's
  dialect below.
- **Pillar 1, Micro-drama (heart / reach).** Daily 30-60s dramatised emotional
  shorts (see `GTM-STORY-BANK.md`). Job: pull, brand, top-of-funnel. Localised
  face + world + register.
- **Pillar 2, Use-case (proof / authority).** Daily. Real enforcement cases
  (Danske, 1MDB, Lazarus, Tornado Cash, Westpac) and exam scenarios told as "here
  is what happened / here is the call you'd have to make." Job: prove depth, earn
  trust, show we teach judgment. This is where the dumps-versus-judgment argument
  lives.
- **Pillar 3, Cards (utility / frequency).** One or two single-frame cards a day:
  a red-flag, a definition, a myth-buster, a "flag it or not?" decision, a real
  candidate quote, a stat. From the glossary/quiz bank. Job: cheap, high-frequency,
  saveable, keeps the brand in feed.

Cadence (solo-sustainable): one commercial per market per launch; ~3-4
micro-dramas/week; a daily use-case; 1-2 cards/day. Every piece points at the free
simulation.

## Where to point it (weighting, from the audience research)

- **Lead hard: Gulf + South Asia.** Sharpest pain, real willingness-to-pay, your
  network and cultural fluency. Most of the effort here.
- **Challenger-wedge: US / UK.** ACAMS's fortress, so play the underdog, not the
  leader. Lighter touch, aimed at the crumb (retakers, self-funders, dump-refusers).
- **Opportunistic: Singapore/HK, Nigeria, Canada, Philippines.** Own them with the
  right dialect while competition is thin.

## The markets

Each: the hero **Commercial** (concept + tagline), then how the three daily pillars
speak in that dialect, with an example of each.

### 🇮🇳 India + 🇧🇩🇵🇰🇱🇰 South Asia, the family / promise
- **Commercial, "The Promise."** A young banker studies past midnight; flashes of
  the family that went without so he could sit this exam. He passes, and he does not
  look at the score, he looks at his mother. Tagline: *"You're not sitting an exam.
  You're keeping a promise."*
- **Micro-drama:** warm, sentiment reaches the eyes. A father silently sets tea by
  the books at 2am and leaves without a word.
- **Use-case:** frame the case as duty. *"1MDB: the same money walked into six
  banks. Five looked away. Which one would you have been?"*
- **Card (mentor tone):** *"Placement, layering, integration is not a law. It's a
  story. Here's the law they'll actually test you on."*

### 🇺🇸 USA, the individual comeback
- **Commercial, "The Comeback."** She failed it once, memorised, guessed, lost. Cut
  to her studying differently, learning to *decide*. Exam day: calm. PASS. No family
  chorus, just her and one long breath. Tagline: *"Nobody's coming to tell you
  you're ready. Know it yourself."*
- **Micro-drama:** individual, cinematic grit, the private arena. The retaker
  deletes the dump PDF and opens the real thing.
- **Use-case:** the pro under pressure. *"Eight seconds, a $2M wire, two hops from a
  sanctioned wallet. Go."*
- **Card (confident, direct):** *"Memorise 500 answers, or learn to make one call.
  The exam only asks the second."*

### 🇬🇧 UK, the dry, earned relief
- **Commercial, "Best Be Sure."** A fifteen-year veteran, deadpan, lists everything
  they've caught, then admits one exam has them rattled. Handles it with a cup of
  tea and a wry line. Ends on a small nod, not a cheer. Tagline: *"Best be sure,
  then."*
- **Micro-drama:** understated, witty. The veteran flags half the paper, mutters
  "well, this is humbling," and puts the pen down early.
- **Use-case (dry, clever):** *"Standard Chartered spent years defeating its own
  screening. Funnier than it sounds, right up until it's your name on the notice."*
- **Card (dry myth-buster):** *"Reminder: the practice test lied to you. On purpose.
  Sort of."*

### 🌙 MENA / Gulf, provision + homecoming
- **Commercial, "The Distance."** An expat father in a Gulf tower, a photo of kids
  far away. The nights, the sacrifice. The exam is what makes it worth it. Ends on a
  video call and a homecoming. Tagline: *"Every mile from home has to be worth
  something."* (Time it to Ramadan/Eid, the region's emotional peak.)
- **Micro-drama:** dignified, family, faith-adjacent warmth. He studies after Isha;
  the family waits; the reunion.
- **Use-case (duty, protecting the name):** *"An exchange house you cleared moved a
  ransom. The regulator is asking what you knew. This is that question, before it's
  real."*
- **Card (respectful):** *"A control isn't paperwork. It's the reason your name
  stays clean. Here's the one they test."*

### 🇸🇬 Singapore (+ 🇭🇰 Hong Kong), meritocracy + kiasu
- **Commercial, "The Letters."** A room of certified professionals. One of them
  actually understands; the rest memorised. A scenario question lands and the room
  quietly divides. Tagline: *"Soon everyone will have the letters. The exam decides
  who earned them."*
- **Micro-drama:** crisp, contained. The MRT commute, everyone studying, the quiet
  fear of being the hollow one.
- **Use-case (precise, meritocratic):** *"Real transaction, four rulebooks, one
  judgment. Make the call the exam will make you make."*
- **Card (sharp):** *"Certified is not the same as competent. The exam knows the
  difference. Do you?"*

### 🇳🇬 Nigeria, bold triumph against the odds
- **Commercial, "They Said."** A montage of doubt: too hard, too costly, not for
  people like you. The striver studies, prays, bets on themselves. Passes. The
  community erupts. Tagline: *"Too hard, too costly, not for people like you. Walk
  in and answer all three."*
- **Micro-drama:** bold, vibrant, triumphant, faith woven in. The celebration, the
  call home, "we did it."
- **Use-case (defiant competence):** *"They moved billions and assumed no one was
  sharp enough to see it. Be the one who sees it."*
- **Card (motivational):** *"They gatekeep the exam with a big price and a scary
  story. Here's the part they'd rather you didn't know."*

### 🇨🇦 Canada, belonging + the life you build
- **Commercial, "The Place You Built."** A newcomer, months of quiet grind after
  long days, building a life in a new country. The pass. Not a celebration, a quiet
  belonging. Tagline: *"You came to build a life that's really yours. Make it
  undeniable."*
- **Micro-drama:** warm, humble, inclusive. The modest apartment, the quiet win, a
  small smile. (Canada's CAMS base is heavily new-Canadian, often from the very
  markets above, so this cut can share faces with them.)
- **Use-case (fairness, doing right):** *"Every clean bank has someone who didn't
  look away. In this country, that person is you."*
- **Card (warm):** *"You don't have to be the loudest in the room. Just the one
  who's actually ready."*

### 🇵🇭 Philippines (extension)
Patterns blend the Gulf's provision and India's family with US-style career hunger,
and it is English-speaking, so the emotional content travels almost unchanged.
- **Commercial, "Para sa Pamilya" (for the family).** The striver studying to lift
  the family or go abroad; the OFW dream. Deeply emotional, family-and-faith.
  Tagline: family + the door it opens.
- Pillars follow the Gulf/India dialects; very shareable emotional content, low
  localisation cost.

## Course-agnostic (the machine outlives any one course)

The engine runs on whatever a course contains. CAMS cases and concepts fuel it
today; **CCAS crypto** (Lazarus, Tornado Cash, mixers, the Bitfinex trace) fuels the
crypto-market cut of every pillar and owns a category with zero competition; each
new course you launch is a fresh tank of fuel at near-zero extra content cost,
because the cases and the emotions repeat, only the surface changes.

## Guardrails (carried from the playbook)

- One CTA everywhere: the free simulation.
- Never dramatise a lie: no fake urgency, no invented pass rates. This audience
  audits claims for a living; honesty is the warmth.
- Nominative trademark use only, "CAMS exam prep (independent, not affiliated with
  ACAMS)", on every surface including video descriptions.
- Shoot the register, swap the face, extend the world, ElevenLabs the voice. Keep
  emotional peaks a touch wider so the face-swap holds.
