# GTM: the whole thing, in one place

The front door to Enso's go-to-market. Four docs, one thesis. Read this page first;
open the others when you need the depth.

## The thesis in one breath

The market is real and underserved (a $299 slot sits empty between $13 dumps and
the $2,000+ official package). The gap to revenue is **trust and distribution, not
product**. The weapon is **story**, the product is built on real financial-crime
cases and aimed at a real fear (a hard, expensive exam that rewards judgment, not
recall). The beachhead is the **Gulf + South Asia corridor**, where the exam can
cost half a year's salary and people already pay dump-tutors to gamble. The engine
is **one hero commercial feeding a daily three-pillar content machine**, localized
into each market's emotional dialect the way Piyush Pandey localized to India. The
production line is cheap enough for one person: **BD talent performs the real
emotion, AI swaps the face to the market and builds the world, ElevenLabs supplies
the native voice.**

## Strategy on a page

- **The one truth (every asset, every market):** the exam tests whether you'd make
  the right call, not what you memorised. Dumps train recall; we train judgment.
  Know you're ready before you pay to find out.
- **The funnel:** free Case Mode (no signup) → free full exam simulation (captures
  the email; the readiness score is the "I'm not ready" gut-punch) → $299 course →
  and B2B seat deals to Gulf/South-Asia banks for the volume.
- **The machine:** a hero Commercial per market, then daily **Micro-drama** (heart /
  reach), **Use-case** (proof / authority, the real cases), and **Cards** (utility /
  frequency). One CTA always: the free simulation.
- **The weighting:** lead hard on Gulf + South Asia; play challenger-underdog in the
  US/UK fortress; own Singapore/HK, Nigeria, Canada, Philippines with the right
  dialect.
- **The production line:** shoot the register, swap the face, extend the world,
  ElevenLabs the voice. One grey wall, one SLR, every market.
- **The honest number:** content builds brand and converts individuals; the *volume*
  (e.g. a 1,500-in-90-days target) comes from B2B seats plus the content flywheel,
  not from cold ads. A small budget is fuel for production and for amplifying proven
  winners, never for cold acquisition.
