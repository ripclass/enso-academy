### Step 1 `Evidence item 1: the two grades` (id 'grades', key c)
evidence.observed: "The Asia/Pacific Group on Money Laundering's Mutual Evaluation Report of Bangladesh, adopted in September 2016, grades the framework on two scales. Technical compliance is substantially built: solid ratings across much of the standard, with Recommendation 6, the terrorism-related targeted-financial-sanctions standard, rated Compliant. Effectiveness reads differently: Immediate Outcome 4, the preventive-measures outcome covering whether financial institutions and DNFBPs actually apply their CDD, reporting and control obligations, is rated Low."
evidence.source: "The public record of the assessment documents."
evidence.inference: "One framework, two scales, and they disagree. What that disagreement actually says is the first call."
evidence.confidence: "High; both ratings are stated in the published report."
decision.prompt: "You brief a regional banking group's country-risk committee on the 2016 report. The chair asks: what does a Compliant on the sanctions standard alongside a Low on preventive measures actually tell us? Commit to a reading."
- a: "That effectiveness ratings grade the statutes: a Low on the preventive-measures outcome means the assessors found the underlying laws deficient, so the committee should expect the framework to be rewritten before any rating moves."
- b: "That the ratings grade the authorities, not the institutions: a Low on Immediate Outcome 4 is a finding about the supervisor's examination program, and it carries no consequence for how an individual bank's obligations are set or examined."
- c (KEYED): "That the laws are substantially built and the sanctions transmission chain worked on paper and in rating, while the preventive obligations were being applied unevenly across institutions: the gap is implementation, and it is graded where it lives, in institution-level files and controls."
- d: "That the report is internally inconsistent: a jurisdiction cannot rate Compliant on a Recommendation while rating Low on an outcome that depends on it, so the committee should discount the assessment and commission its own country review."
decision.explanation: "The two scales measure different things by design, and the disagreement is the finding. Technical compliance asks whether the laws and instruments exist as the standard requires; effectiveness asks whether the system produces the outcomes the standard is for. A framework can therefore be genuinely Compliant on the sanctions standard, the statutory transmission machinery in place and rated, while preventive measures rate Low because CDD, reporting and controls are unevenly applied across a large, cash-heavy banking system and a barely-supervised DNFBP sector. The statutes-are-deficient reading imports effectiveness into the wrong scale: the technical ratings already said the laws are substantially built. The authorities-only reading misses where Immediate Outcome 4 is graded: it grades preventive measures at financial institutions and DNFBPs, which is precisely why it reaches banks. And the inconsistency reading mistakes the methodology's whole point: the two scales are kept separate exactly so that paper and practice can be graded apart."
reveal: "The report's own pattern is the global one this course has met in every framework: law outrunning implementation, rendered at developing-economy scale. The 2016 ratings rest on the statute-and-instruction chain of their time; the consolidated circular this lesson teaches came later, in June 2020, carrying that machinery forward. What the assessment did next is the second item."

### Step 2 `Evidence item 2: the follow-up` (id 'followup', key b)
evidence.observed: "The 2016 assessment places Bangladesh in enhanced follow-up. Across the following years the record shows legislative and regulatory repairs, beneficial-ownership strengthening, supervisory tightening, and the consolidation of the banking sector's instructions into BFIU Circular No. 26 in June 2020. The 4th Enhanced Follow-Up Report, published in November 2020, records re-ratings."
evidence.source: "The public record of the assessment documents."
evidence.inference: "A national report card, and then years of repairs. Whether any of it reaches an individual bank's desk is the second call."
evidence.confidence: "High; the follow-up sequence and the re-rating record are published."
decision.prompt: "A newly appointed CAMLCO in Dhaka reads the follow-up record and asks you, the group's regional head: what does a national assessment cycle actually mean for my bank's next examination? Commit."
- a: "Formally, nothing: mutual evaluation is a state-to-state exercise between the assessment body and the government, and its findings create no obligations and change no supervisory behaviour at the level of an individual institution."
- b (KEYED): "The report is the supervisor's published to-do list: the preventive-measures finding is graded in branch KYC files, profile quality, report narratives and screening logs, so examination intensity, prescribed self-assessment and the circular consolidation are the follow-up process transmitted downward to your desk."
- c: "It means relisting is imminent: an outcome rated Low that persists past one follow-up cycle returns the jurisdiction to FATF's increased-monitoring list mechanically, so the bank should prepare for the correspondent de-risking that listing triggers."
- d: "It means the local framework cannot yet be relied on: until the next full evaluation re-grades effectiveness, the bank should set local requirements aside and run the group's home-country standard as the operative rulebook."
decision.explanation: "The transmission is the point. A preventive-measures finding is not graded in ministries; it is graded in the files of the institutions the outcome covers, which is why the years after the report look the way they do from a Dhaka desk: examination intensity rises, self-assessment is prescribed, the instructions consolidate. The state-to-state reading describes the ceremony and misses the machinery: supervisors answer for the national rating with the tools they have, and their tools all point at banks. The mechanical-relisting reading invents a trigger the process does not contain: listing decisions run through their own review, and this jurisdiction's trajectory ran through follow-up, not listing. And the run-the-home-standard reading repeats the cold open's error in reverse: local law does not switch off while it is being improved, and the operative rulebook remains the one the supervisor examines against."
reveal: "The record shows exactly that transmission: re-ratings secured through legislative and regulatory repair, the 2020 circular consolidation carrying the machinery forward, and the effectiveness agenda, supervision depth, DNFBP coverage, the conviction-and-confiscation pipeline, named as the longer climb. Where the trajectory lands is the prediction."

The 2016 report triggered enhanced follow-up; the November 2020 report records re-ratings; the effectiveness agenda remains open. The next full evaluation round will grade the same outcomes again."
evidence.source: "The public record of the assessment documents."
evidence.inference: "A decade of trajectory: exit, assessment, repair, re-rating. Before the reveal, call where the jurisdiction stands on FATF's public lists."
evidence.confidence: "High for the published sequence; the prediction is yours."
decision.prompt: "Before the reveal: on the current public record, where does Bangladesh stand with FATF's listing machinery, and why?"
- a (KEYED): "Not on FATF's public lists: the record shows a framework holding its standing through the follow-up machinery rather than the listing machinery, re-ratings secured through repair, with the effectiveness agenda still described as the longer climb rather than as a listing trigger."
- b: "Back under increased monitoring: a preventive-measures outcome rated Low that outlasts a follow-up cycle meets the listing criteria, and the standard pattern for developing economies is a return to the grey list at a subsequent plenary."
- c: "Re-rated effective: the November 2020 follow-up report's re-ratings closed the Immediate Outcome 4 finding, so the framework now grades as effective on preventive measures and the assessment cycle is complete until the next round."
- d: "Subject to a call for action: the combination of a cash-heavy economy and a Low preventive-measures outcome moved the jurisdiction onto the list that carries counter-measures, pending demonstration of effectiveness at the next evaluation."
decision.explanation: "Follow the machinery, not the vibe. The listing process and the follow-up process are different tracks, and this record runs down the second: repairs, re-ratings, standing held. The relisting answer invents a mechanical trigger and misreads the pattern it appeals to: jurisdictions return to the list through the listing process's own review, not because an effectiveness rating aged past a cycle. The re-rated-effective answer confuses the scales one last time: follow-up re-ratings move technical-compliance ratings, while effectiveness is re-graded at evaluation, so an Immediate Outcome finding stays open precisely until the next round grades it. And the call-for-action answer reaches for the wrong list entirely: counter-measures attach to the highest-risk tier, a categorically different finding from an effectiveness gap under repair."
reveal: "Bangladesh is not on FATF's current public lists. The trajectory is the file's closing exhibit: a framework that entered the decade through the listing machinery and holds its standing through the follow-up machinery, with the daily work this course trains, KYC files, profile quality, report narratives, screening logs, as the national rating's raw material."

debrief: "Read the file back as the module's closing argument. Second, the assessment cycle is a national compliance program: grey-list exit, evaluation, follow-up, re-rating, the same remediate-and-evidence loop this course taught at institutional scale, run by a state against the clock. That is true in every jurisdiction; the Bangladesh record simply shows it in public, with dates."
