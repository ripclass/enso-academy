## Scene 3 — `The decision standard` (NEW slide, callout)
item: "Programs fail at the top before they fail at any screen. Walk the five components in order, management commitment, risk assessment, internal controls, testing and auditing, training, and ask one question of each: do the paper, the budget and the behaviour tell the same story? Design rule: prohibition decisions never queue behind suspicion workflows. Breach rule: contain, preserve, scope honestly, know whose clocks are running, and decide disclosure at governance level, on a record. Silence by drift is the one indefensible outcome."

narration: "Here is the standard behind the call you just made, and every decision in this lesson reapplies it. Governance first: the enforcement record this module built, the overrides, the unowned architecture, the controls managed around growth, is a record of programs failing at the top before any screen failed, which is why OFAC's Framework opens with management commitment and why your budget answer was really a question about authority. Then the walk: five components, one test each, does the paper match the budget, and does the budget match the behaviour? A risk assessment is honest or it is shelf-ware. Controls operate as written or they are paper. Testing is designed to find failures before the examiner does, or it is theatre. Training is role-specific and measured, or it is a slideshow that later proves what the institution knew. One design rule runs beneath it: sanctions and AML share a substrate but run on different engines, and a prohibition decision must never queue behind a suspicion workflow. And when it breaks, the sequence is fixed: contain, preserve, scope honestly, know whose clocks are already running, then decide disclosure at governance level, on counsel's advice, on a record. Silence by drift is the one outcome no record can defend."

### Step 1 `Evidence item 1: what December 2012 actually bought` (id 'obligation', key a)
evidence.observed: "August 2012: the New York State Department of Financial Services orders the bank to answer for its Iran-related dollar-clearing practices, in an order characterising the institution as having operated as a 'rogue institution'; the matter settles for USD 340 million. December 2012: a further USD 327 million combined resolution with federal and New York authorities, alongside a deferred prosecution agreement with the Department of Justice: charges suspended on conditions, remediation, cooperation, clean conduct, with a term measured in years."
evidence.source: "The public record of the resolution documents."
evidence.inference: "The fines are paid and the headlines fade. What the institution actually carries out of December 2012 is the question that decides the next seven years."
evidence.confidence: "High; the orders, settlements and the deferred prosecution agreement are the binding public record."
decision.prompt: "The general counsel briefs the board in January 2013. Which reading of the bank's position is the disciplined one?"
- a (KEYED): "The resolution is the beginning of an obligation: the remediation commitments are enforceable on their own terms, supervisors will test them, and the suspended charges make every future gap more expensive than the first ones. The program the bank promised is now the program the law expects."
- b: "The matter is priced and closed: the penalties bought finality on the conduct, remediation is now an internal improvement programme on the bank's own schedule, and the deferred prosecution agreement will lapse quietly at term if no new sanctions violations occur."
- c: "The agreement is protective: while the deferred prosecution agreement runs, the bank cannot be pursued again over the same compliance program, so its practical effect is a multi-year shield in exchange for the penalties the bank has already paid."
- d: "The exposure is now reputational: the legal risk has been capped by settlement, and the board's residual task is managing headline risk and client attrition while the compliance function absorbs the remediation work at its own pace."
explanation: "A deferred prosecution agreement suspends charges on conditions, and the conditions are the point: remediation written into a resolution document is an enforceable obligation with supervisors intending to test it, not an internal project plan. That is why the disciplined reading treats December 2012 as the start of the clock. The priced-and-closed reading mistakes leverage for finality: the charges are suspended, not spent, and the schedule belongs to the conditions rather than the bank. The shield reading inverts what a deferred prosecution agreement is: it is the authorities' leverage over the institution, held open precisely so that performance can be compelled, and it precludes nothing if the conditions fail. And the reputational reading misses that awareness is now established: after a resolution, every gap the institution knows about and leaves open is judged with that knowledge attached, which is the opposite of capped exposure."
reveal: "The bank left December 2012 holding suspended charges, enforceable remediation commitments, and supervisors intending to test them. The next event on the arc is the test."

### Step 2 `Evidence item 2: the remediation is examined` (id 'commitment', key b)
evidence.observed: "Two years into the deferred prosecution agreement, the supervisor examines the remediation the bank committed to in 2012. The transaction-surveillance systems the bank undertook to fix are found still deficient. No new sanctions violations are on the table; the finding is about the remediation itself."
evidence.source: "The public record of the resolution documents."
evidence.inference: "The category of this event is the hinge of the lesson: what force does a remediation commitment carry once it is written into a resolution?"
evidence.confidence: "High; the 2014 supervisory action and its basis are the documented public record."
decision.prompt: "The finding lands on the board table. What is the bank's exposure?"
- a: "None yet: enforcement attaches to violations, and with no new sanctions breach found, a shortfall against an improvement plan is a supervisory dialogue item, to be handled through revised milestones and a refreshed workplan rather than through penalties."
- b (KEYED): "Full enforcement exposure on the commitment itself: remediation promised in a resolution is an enforceable obligation, the supervisor can price the gap between promised and delivered, and the deferred prosecution agreement's conditions are now in question, with everything that implies."
- c: "Exposure only if causation is shown: a deficient surveillance system matters legally only if a specific prohibited payment passed through the gap, so the bank should require the supervisor to identify missed transactions before it accepts any penalty."
- d: "A monitorship matter, not an enforcement one: findings of this kind route to the monitor and the remediation workplan, and converting a progress shortfall into a public penalty would undercut the supervisory relationship the 2012 settlement was designed to create."
explanation: "This is the hinge the whole arc turns on: a remediation commitment in a resolution document is itself an enforceable obligation, and the supervisor tested it and priced the gap. No new violation is required, the same principle the screening lesson met at Commerzbank, where the condition of a control was independently enforceable. The dialogue-item and monitorship readings make the same category error from different directions, demoting an obligation to a plan; plans get revised milestones, obligations get enforcement. The causation reading imports a violation requirement the commitment does not carry: the bank promised working surveillance, and the promise, not a missed payment, is what the supervisor measured. And sitting beneath all three wrong answers is the fact the reveal prices: the suspended charges mean the institution performs or the agreement's whole logic turns against it."
reveal: "USD 300 million, plus business restrictions, for the gap between the remediation promised and the remediation delivered. And the deferred prosecution agreement did not lapse at term: it was extended repeatedly, each extension a public statement that the conditions were not yet met."
